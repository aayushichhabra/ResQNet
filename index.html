Introduction & Risks

  • India faces frequent disasters such as floods, earthquakes, landslides and cyclones.
  • During emergencies, the Golden Hour (first 60 minutes) is crucial for saving lives.
  • However, communication breakdown causes delayed rescue operations.
  • ResQNet provides an offline-first disaster response platform for citizens, NGOs and government.
  • India is the 3rd most disaster-prone country globally.
  • Over ₹4.3 Trillion economic loss occurred between 2014–2024.

Major failures happen due to:

  • power grid failure
  • cellular collapse
  • no real-time victim tracking

ResQNet ensures rescue coordination even during network blackout.

Literature Review

Existing Disaster Management Systems

Geological Survey of India — Bhukamp Tool

  • Earthquake hazard mapping and seismic zone analysis
  • Used for risk prediction and planning infrastructure safety
  • Not designed for direct citizen rescue communication

National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)

  • Government-led disaster monitoring and alert dissemination platforms
  • Provide cyclone, flood, earthquake and heatwave warnings
  • Use SMS alerts, web dashboards, and official advisories

Google Crisis Response

  • Provides real-time disaster maps and emergency information
  • Integrates evacuation routes, shelters, and safe locations
  • Used globally during earthquakes, wildfires, floods, pandemics

Limitations Identified in Existing Systems

1. Internet Dependency | 2. One-Way Communication | 3. Lack of Real-Time Ground Feedback

Research Gap

Focus of Current Solutions

Most existing disaster platforms mainly support:

  • Disaster prediction → early warnings (cyclones, earthquakes, floods)
  • Post-relief reporting → damage assessment & official updates

No solution works efficiently in the blackout phase.

Most systems lack:

  • SOS clustering
  • offline reporting
  • real-time heatmaps
  • last-mile victim tracking

Key Findings from Study

  • Traditional systems depend on high-bandwidth networks.
  • Communication becomes unreliable during the first few hours.
  • Victims cannot share location → rescuers lack visibility.

Need for a platform that enables:

  • bilateral crowdsourcing
  • offline-first reporting
  • centralized dashboard visibility
PROBLEM STATEMENT

During disasters, internet and cellular failures create an information blackout, preventing citizens from sending SOS signals and stopping NGOs/government from locating victims during the Golden Hour.

CORE OBJECTIVES

REAL TIME CLUSTERING

Provide real-time clustering + heatmaps for better rescue prioritization.

AI PREDICTION

Predict required resources (ambulances, rescue teams, fire vans) using AI.

OFFLINE FIRST

Build an offline-first SOS reporting system that works without 4G/5G.

UNIFIED PLATFORM

Create a unified platform connecting Citizens, NGOs and Government.

Step 2: User SOS Reporting

RESQNET APP

Purpose: Instant Distress Communication

Features & Live Deployment (Deployed APK):

  • One-tap Emergency SOS (no login)
  • Auto GPS location sharing
  • Incident selection (flood / fire / landslide)
  • Works in offline blackout mode
  • Stores data → sends when signal returns

App Successfully Deployed: ResQNet is now a fully functional Android APK distributed for real-world use.

  • Home Screen Features: Live GPS coordinates displayed on launch, Real-time weather + temperature from device location, Emergency SOS button — broadcasts instantly to rescue teams, Live Updates panel for active risks in your area.
  • SOS Beacon System: One-tap SOS sends an emergency beacon directly to the NGO dashboard. Works from the lock screen — no login required. Confirmation shown: 'Emergency beacon broadcasted successfully to Rescue Teams.'
  • Widget on Home Screen: SEND SOS widget available directly on Android home screen for fastest access.

Key Idea: Victim should never be digitally invisible.

➡ Help request sent even without internet

Step 3: Rescue Procedure

NGO DASHBOARD

Purpose: Rescue Team Decision Center

Features (GPS Routing + History & Audit Log):

  • Receives live SOS signals
  • Groups nearby victims (SOS clustering)
  • Shows danger heatmap
  • Suggests rescue action (boat / ambulance / team)
  • Turn-by-Turn GPS Navigation: Clicking any incident on the map shows the exact route from rescue base to victim. Live estimated time: e.g. 25.8 km — 23 min via Ajmer Road / NH48. Step-by-step directions help field teams navigate blocked or unfamiliar roads.
  • Incident History & Audit Log: Full timeline of all incidents — Completed, Dispatched, or Pending. Each entry shows disaster type (Flood, SOS, Fire, Landslide) and message. Downloadable Audit Log (CSV) for post-disaster review and compliance.
  • Live Resource Counter: Dashboard header shows live count of Ambulances, Fire Units & Volunteers. AI Live Predictor recalculates resource needs as new reports come in.
  • Resolve / Dispatch Actions: Each incident card has Resolve (close) or Dispatch (send team) buttons. Resolved incidents automatically move to History.

Key Idea: Not just seeing disaster, knowing who needs help first.

➡ Faster and smarter rescue deployment

P2P MESH NETWORKING
NEW FEATURE

● Online: Active & scanning for peers

○ Offline: Broadcasting SOS report to mesh

What is Mesh Networking?

Devices communicate directly with each other using Bluetooth & WiFi-Direct — no internet required.

How ResQNet Uses It

  • When offline, the app broadcasts SOS reports to nearby ResQNet peers.
  • Each peer relays the signal further, creating a chain of communication.
  • Reports hop from device to device until they reach an area with connectivity.

Key Stats Shown

  • Nearby Peers: real-time count of connected devices in range.
  • Broadcasting: number of reports being actively relayed.
  • Received: reports received from neighbouring peers.

Impact: Victims can call for help even in a total network blackout — the mesh carries the signal.
